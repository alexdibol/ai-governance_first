Governance-First Collection

A six + one collection for regulated professional practice in finance and law: six Applied AI volumes plus one foundational Machine Learning volume. The purpose is durable governance that survives model change: Capability increases → Risk increases → Controls MUST increase. Each book is paired with Colab notebooks so students implement governance patterns (scope, verification discipline, audit artifacts, review gates) rather than merely read about them. The author is also the instructor and accompanies students through the journey.

What You Get (Across the Entire Collection)

The common spine of every volume is governance that remains valid as models evolve. The books intentionally focus on durable discipline, not transient UI features. Each volume includes classroom-ready, executable notebooks aligned to the book’s structure.

  • Governance-first mental model: generation is separated from verification; outputs are treated as Not verified until validated by a human.
  • Scope and boundary control: clear task definitions, forbidden behaviors, refusal posture, and “stop-if” rules when facts are missing.
  • Auditability by design: repeatable artifacts and logs so work can be reconstructed and reviewed, not merely observed.
  • Professional defensibility: designed for supervision, documentation, and regulated workflows (finance + law).
  • Instructor-led execution: each book is paired with Colab notebooks so students practice the discipline end-to-end.
  • Obsolescence resistance: the structure is model-agnostic—new models can be swapped in without weakening the governance frame.
Why “six + one”? The domains differ, but the governance discipline is unified. The Machine Learning foundation is the deliberate starting point: it builds governance posture before GenAI workflows. The six Applied AI volumes extend the same discipline into regulated practice, and the Fine-Tuning volume extends it into training itself.

License and Disclaimers

Educational disclaimer: This collection is provided for educational purposes only. It does not constitute investment, legal, tax, accounting, compliance, or financial advice. Qualified human professionals must review, verify, and approve any use in practice.

Client confidentiality and data hygiene: Do not paste confidential client information into external model prompts. Use redaction/anonymization and “minimum necessary” inputs by default. Document any exception and follow firm policy and applicable law.

Facts are not assumptions: Outputs must clearly separate facts provided by the user from assumptions and open questions. Treat all model-generated content as Not verified until validated by a human.

No fabricated sources or product claims: Zero tolerance for invented product terms, performance claims, fees, tax consequences, or citations. When evidence is missing, the correct output is a verification task list.
